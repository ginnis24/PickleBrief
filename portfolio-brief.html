How to read a strategy card

Each card shows a rule for choosing what to hold. A pool of candidate tickers gets sorted by some signal — momentum, RSI, distance from highs, analyst upside — and the rule picks the top few to hold, usually equal-weight.

The big number top-rightis CAGR — the strategy's annualized return over the trailing year. The colored chip below the name (Aggressive, Defensive, etc.) flags how the strategy behaves.

The middle bandshows what the rule selects today. Tickers, weights, and each pick's score by the chosen signal — that's the allocation if you actually followed the rule right now.

The four-stat row at the bottomis how the strategy did over the last 12 months: vs SPY in basis points, max drawdown, Sharpe ratio, and win rate. Tap any underlined term to learn what it means; the full glossary is the button up top.

"Modeled · no costs"means these backtests are reasoned approximations, not audited engine runs. They're directional — useful for comparing strategies against each other, less useful as exact return predictions.
